Company Fuego Flamenco

The vision of Fuego Flamenco is to continue to present modern influences without compromising traditions of pure flamenco, giving opportunities to new talents, both local and international. Fuego Flamenco has performed at Casa Panza, New World Symphony with Carmen Linares, Boston Flamenco Ballet with Jose Greco, Bailes Ferrer in Ft. Lauderdale and at the Hall Center with other ballets.


Estrella Morena

(Dancer)

Known worldwide amount Flamenco aficionados Estrella Morena of Madrid made her debut at the age of 16 as “bailaora” of flamenco at the famous Tablao LOS CANASTEROS of Manolo Caracol. At 16 she was also soloist at the Tablao TORRES BERMEJAS. Shortly thereafter, she made her debut as principal dancer at the great TEATRO DE LA ZARZUELA in Madrid, and later as principal dancer at the tablao DUENDE for a period of 2 years. Estrella began her training and choreography in 1969 at a very young age, at the same time she became principal dancer with the Great Antonio, where she received training from some of the best teachers and choreographers from Madrid, which today have become master teachers and choreographers with the National Ballet of Spain. In 1985 Estrella moved with her family to Miami where she established herself as an artist alongside her husband, Pepe de Malaga, and her son, Jose Moreno, where they have collectively performed successfully in some of the best flamenco tablaos in Miami. Pepe de Málaga

(Flamenco Singer)

Born in Costa del Sol, Malaga, Pepe de Malaga moved to Madrid at a very young age where he debuted as a cataor in the tablao Las Brujas. During this time, he gained his stage name. He has since been associated with renowned flamenco figures such as Fosforito, Antonio Molina, Marife de Triana, Rafael Farina, Juanita Reina and Caracolillo. In 1964 and 1973, he won two First Place awards Del Cante Flamenco singing in the Saetas form. As the cataor for the Ballet of Antonio and Rafael de Cordova, he toured all over Europe with great success. Thereafter he joined the company of Estrella Morena as exclusive cataor and once again toured all over Spain, Asia, Europe, and the US. Since then, he has been presented at an honorary gala for the King and Queenof Spain at the Waldorf Astoria in New York, the Chamber of Commerce of Spain , and the Casa de Espana of New York, Carnegie Hall, Chateu Madrid, the Spanish Institute Hunter College, and the Barbizon Plaza. He has completed lengthy seasons in the Miami, FL and in 1984 was featured in a special presentation honoring Placido Domingo for hi movie "Carmen.". Most recently he sang at the Lincoln Theater in Miami Beach for Estrella Morena with the New Worlds Symphony and the renowned flamenco artist Carmen Linares. He has also performed as a special guest artist for Bailes Ferrer Flamenco music and dance, Irene Rimer and Ballet Flamenco La Rosa.

Jose Moreno

(Dancer / Percussionist)

Born into a family of famous flamenco artists Estrella Morena (dancer) and Pepe de Málaga (singer), Jose began his flamenco career at age 6 under the guidance of his parents. His debut was at the famous Tablao Costa Vasca in Miami. Jose continued his studies with the Great Manolete, Farruquito, and Andres Marin and Cajon with Manuel Soler. Jose has been invited to perform in various companies, and worked with distinguished artists such as: The Great Manolete, Joaquin Ruiz, Pastora Galvan, El Pecas, Jose Cortes “Pansequito”, Jose Luis Rodriquez, Roberto Castellon, Pedro Cortes, Basilio Garcia, Paco Heredia, Jesus Montoya, La Tania, Miguel Vargas, Nelida Tirado, Maria Montilla, Antonio Hidalgo, Curro Cueto, Ruben de Maria, Gonzalo Grau, Edwin Aparicio, Amparo Heredia, Antonio Granjero and Omayra Amaya. In 2001, Jose choreographed and performed a collaborative work with his mother Estrella Morena, and with renowned flamenco singer Carmen Linares with the New World Symphony Orquestra at Lincoln Theater in Miami. In 2007, Jose performed with the internationally known Isabel Pantoja as a cajón percussionist, alongside flamenco guitarist Jose de Luna. Also performed in the 7th annual Panama Jazz Festival, and Carnegie Hall and currently resides in New York City. www.josemorenoflamenco.com
